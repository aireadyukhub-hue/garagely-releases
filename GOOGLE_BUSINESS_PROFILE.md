# Google Business Profile — Setup Content

Ready to paste in at business.google.com. This is what actually makes searching "GarageDash" (or
"GarageDash reviews", "GarageDash pricing") show a rich result at the top of Google fast — branded
search is much quicker to win than competitive terms like "garage management software".

## Business type

Set this up as a **service-area business** (no public storefront/address) rather than a physical
location — GarageDash is software, not a shop people visit. During setup:
- "Do you have a location customers can visit?" → **No**
- "Service area" → United Kingdom

## Category

- Primary: **Software Company**
- Additional (if offered): Business Management Consultant, or Computer Software Store — pick
  whichever Google suggests closest to "software company" in your account; primary category
  matters most.

```
GarageDash is garage management software built for independent UK garages and mechanics. It covers
bookings, job sheets, quotes, invoices, customer and vehicle records, parts stock, and digital
vehicle-health-check inspections with photo attachments — on desktop (Windows) and in the browser.
GarageDash is priced per garage, not per user: £29/month covers your whole team with no per-seat
fees, and includes a simplified Technician Mode for workshop staff. Every new account starts with
a 14-day free trial, no card required. Built in the UK for UK garages — VAT handling, MOT/service
reminders, and OBD-II fault-code lookup included.
```

## Q&A section (Google shows a public Q&A — seed it yourself so accurate answers show first)

**Q: How much does GarageDash cost?**
A: £29 per month per garage, with unlimited users included. No per-technician fees.

**Q: Is there a free trial?**
A: Yes — 14 days free, no card required to start.

**Q: Does it work on Mac?**
A: The web app works on any device including Mac. A native Mac desktop app is coming soon; Windows
is available now.

**Q: Is this only for big garages?**
A: No — GarageDash is built for independent garages and small workshops specifically, priced to suit
a single-site operation.

## After it's live

- Ask a couple of early customers for a genuine Google review — reviews are one of the strongest
  ranking signals for local/branded search, and there's no substitute for real ones.
- Keep the profile "active" — Google deprioritises profiles with no posts/updates for months, so a
  short update post (new feature, a review reply) every few weeks helps.
