## First two weeks — content calendar

One post every 2–3 days is plenty to start; consistency matters more than volume. Each entry is a
concept + a draft caption — adapt the tone once you see how your audience responds.

**Post 1 — Launch announcement**
> Meet GarageDash 🔧 Garage management software built for UK garages — bookings, job sheets,
> invoices, customers, vehicles, all in one place. £29/month, unlimited users, no per-seat fees.
> 14-day free trial, no card needed. Link in bio.

**Post 2 — The problem (relatable pain point)**
> Still running your garage on paper job sheets and a diary? We built GarageDash because that was
> costing us hours every week. One system, one login for the whole team, £29/month flat.

**Post 3 — Feature: Digital inspections**
> Vehicle health checks, digitised. Pass / Advisory / Fail per item, photos attached, and a branded
> report your customer can see on their phone — no more scribbled checklists.

**Post 4 — Feature: Bookings calendar**
> See your whole week at a glance. Colour-coded by technician, holidays and time off built in — no
> more double-booked bays.

**Post 5 — Pricing honesty**
> No per-technician fees. No hidden add-ons. £29/month covers your whole garage, however many people
> are using it. That's the whole pricing page.

**Post 6 — Feature: Technician Mode**
> Your techs don't need to see pricing or reports — just today's bookings, the job sheet, and the
> inspection form. Technician Mode gives them exactly that, on a shared workshop tablet.

**Post 7 — Social proof / behind the scenes**
> [Once you have a real customer quote or screenshot, swap this in — testimonials outperform
> feature posts by a wide margin. Even a WhatsApp screenshot of positive feedback works.]

**Post 8 — Tips post (value-first, not a sales post)**
> Quick tip for garage owners: MOT and service reminders save more repeat business than most people
> think. GarageDash flags them automatically on your dashboard — but even a spreadsheet reminder beats
> nothing.

**Post 9 — Feature: Quotes → Job → Invoice**
> Quote it, convert it to a job sheet, convert that to an invoice — the same line items carry
> through automatically. No retyping a job three times.

**Post 10 — Trial CTA (direct)**
> 14 days free, no card needed. If GarageDash doesn't save you time in the first week, it's not for
> you — but most garages know within a day. Link in bio.

## Notes on tone

Keep it plain and practical — UK garage owners respond better to "this saves you admin time" than
to hype. Avoid stock-photo corporate language; the brand voice so far (from the app itself, e.g.
"Timmy the 10mm" assistant) is a bit cheeky/friendly, not corporate — carry that into captions
where it fits.
