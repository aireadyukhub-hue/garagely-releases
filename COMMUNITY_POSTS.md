# Community posts — UK garage owner spaces

Written 7 Jul 2026. Founder-voice, value-first, never spam. Nothing here says "new" or "just launched."

## Where

- **Facebook groups** — search "garage owners UK", "UK independent garage owners", "mobile mechanics UK", "MOT testers UK". Join with your personal profile, read the rules (many ban promo posts but allow replies/recommendations), lurk 2–3 days before posting.
- **Garage Wire forum** — garagewire.co.uk (free registration; independent garage audience, also read by trade press).
- **IGA member forum** — independentgarageassociation.co.uk (requires IGA membership; consider joining — membership itself is a trust signal you can put on the site).
- **Mechanics-adjacent Reddit** — r/mechanics, r/MechanicAdvice have UK members; strict self-promo rules, reply-only.

## Golden rules

1. Reply > post. The best converting move is answering someone's "what software do you all use?" thread.
2. Declare yourself every time: "full disclosure — I run GarageDash." Undeclared shilling gets found out and torches the brand in a small industry.
3. Give the whole answer including competitors. Recommending TechMan for a 20-bay workshop earns the right to recommend yourself for the 2-bay one.
4. One value post per group per month, max. Unlimited helpful replies.

## Reply template — "what software do you use?" threads

> Full disclosure, I run one of the options so I'm biased — but here's the honest lay of the land:
>
> - TechMan — most established, from ~£189/mo, usually 3-year contracts. Good for bigger workshops.
> - Garage Hive — popular with 3–8 bay shops, but the Microsoft licence on top means real cost is per-user.
> - AutoChain — £69.99/mo, no contract, decent for small independents.
> - GDS — from ~£149/mo plus setup fee.
> - Mine is GarageDash — £29/mo flat for the whole garage, no per-user fees, no contract. Built for 1–4 bay independents and mobile mechanics.
>
> Honest answer: if you're a bigger operation with complex parts integrations, look at the top of that list. If you're a small independent still on paper or fed up with per-user pricing, that's exactly who I built for. Happy to answer questions either way — and if anyone wants to try it I'll set you up personally, no card details needed.

## Value post — MOT reminders (use in FB groups / Garage Wire)

> Rough numbers I keep seeing from independent garages: a single bay loses hundreds a month just from MOT lapses — customers who'd book with you but forget the date and end up wherever Google sends them.
>
> Whatever software you use (or a spreadsheet — no judgement), a simple reminder workflow fixes most of it: capture MOT due date at every job, text at 4 weeks and 1 week, mention next year's MOT on the invoice.
>
> I run GarageDash (garage management software, so declared interest) and wrote up the full workflow here if useful: garagedash.co.uk/blog/mot-reminder-workflows.html — but the process works with any system.

## Value post — the pricing one (highest risk, highest reward; use sparingly)

> Question for garage owners: what do you actually pay per month for your management software, all-in — including per-user fees, SMS credits, lookups, the Microsoft licence if you're on Hive?
>
> I ask because I run a software company in this space (GarageDash, £29 flat) and the #1 thing I hear is people discovering their "£150/month" system is really £300+ once everything's counted. Genuinely curious what the real numbers look like out there.

This one starts conversations and surfaces frustrated buyers — but only post it where self-ID'd vendors are tolerated. If a group bans it, don't argue; the reply template is always safe.

## What NOT to do

- No "excited to announce", no "we've just launched", no rocket emojis.
- Don't post the same text in multiple groups on the same day (screenshots travel).
- Don't argue with competitors' fans. "Fair — it's a solid product. Different garages, different needs" wins the room.
- Don't DM people who haven't engaged with you first (most groups treat unsolicited DMs as bannable spam).
