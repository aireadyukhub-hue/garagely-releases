# GarageDash Outreach Kit — first paying customers

Written 7 Jul 2026. Goal: 10 real conversations with UK garage owners → first 3–5 customers → first 5 reviews. No "we just launched" framing anywhere — GarageDash presents as an established product with confident pricing.

**The private lever:** the public trial requires a card. You can remotely issue cardless trials. Never advertise this on the site — use it person-to-person: "I'll set you up directly, no card needed." It reads as personal service, not desperation, and it removes the #1 objection in outreach without touching the public funnel.

---

## Who to target (in order of likely yield)

1. **Garages you already know** — your own MOT/service garage, family, friends-of-friends. Warm beats cold 10:1.
2. **1–4 bay independents still on paper/spreadsheets/Excel** — the "no software yet" segment. They're not comparing you to TechMan; they're comparing you to a diary and a carbonless invoice pad. Easiest sale, most honest feedback.
3. **Garages visibly unhappy with pricing** — anyone mentioning TechMan/GDS/Garage Hive costs in Facebook groups or forums (see COMMUNITY_POSTS.md).
4. **Mobile mechanics** — underserved, price-sensitive, active in Facebook groups, fast decision cycle (one person, no committee).

Skip for now: multi-site groups, franchise dealers, bodyshops (different workflow).

## Phone / walk-in script (the highest-yield channel)

Keep it under 30 seconds before they talk:

> "Hi — I run GarageDash, it's garage management software: bookings, job sheets, invoices, MOT reminders. One flat price, £29 a month for the whole garage, no per-user fees. I'm talking to a handful of independent garages this month to make sure it fits how you actually work. Can I ask — what are you using right now to run the diary and invoicing?"

Then SHUT UP and listen. The goal of the call is the answer to that question, not the sale. Whatever they say:

- Paper/Excel → "That's exactly who it's built for. Want me to set you up with a trial directly — no card, no commitment — and you tell me what's annoying about it?"
- Anyone switching → lead with the onboarding offer: "**Free onboarding assistance from your old software** — send me a CSV export (or photos of the diary, honestly) and I'll have your customers, vehicles and history loaded before you log in the first time." This kills the #1 real switching cost. It's on the site now; say it in every call.
- Existing software → "What does that cost you a month?" (let them say a number 5–10x yours) → "Ours is £29 flat. If you ever want to compare, I'll set you up side-by-side for free."
- Not interested → "No problem. One last thing — what would software have to do for you to bother switching?" (this answer is gold; log it)

## Cold email / Facebook DM template

Subject: **£29/month garage software — quick question**

> Hi [name],
>
> I run GarageDash — garage management software for UK independents: bookings, job sheets, digital vehicle checks, invoices, MOT reminders. One flat £29/month for the whole garage; most systems charge £150–400 and per-user fees on top.
>
> I'm personally onboarding a small number of garages this month. If you're running things on paper or paying too much for software, reply and I'll set you up with a trial directly — no card details needed, and I'll handle the setup with you.
>
> Either way — what do you use to run the workshop today? Genuinely interested.
>
> Lewis
> garagedash.co.uk

Rules: send individually, never bulk. Personalise the first line with something real (their Google reviews, their MOT bay, their town). 10 good DMs beat 100 blasts.

## The ask ladder (per contact, in order)

1. Conversation (what do you use / what does it cost / what's annoying)
2. Cardless trial, personally set up
3. 15-min onboarding call in week 1 of trial ("I'll load your customers in with you")
4. Once they're using it and happy — the review ask (below)
5. Referral: "know another garage owner who'd want this?"

## Review ask (only after they've had a real win)

Trigger moments: first invoice sent, first MOT reminder batch, end of week 1. Ask verbally or:

> "Glad it's working. Quick favour — reviews are how garage owners find software they can trust, and we're on Google [+ Capterra once listed]. Two honest sentences would mean a lot: [GBP review link] "

Never incentivise reviews (violates Google/Capterra terms and reads fake). "Honest" is the operative word — a 4-star review with a niggle is more credible than five empty 5-stars.

## Objection cheat-sheet

- **"What's the catch at £29?"** — "No catch: no setup fee, no per-user fees, no contract. We keep costs low by not running a sales team — which is why I'm calling you myself."
- **"Is my data safe / what if you disappear?"** — "Your data's exportable any time. And you're on a monthly rolling plan — if we ever let you down, you leave with everything."
- **"I don't have time to switch."** — "That's why I do the setup with you. One 15-minute call, I load your customers, you're running."
- **"No Mac app?"** — "Browser version works on Mac today at app.garagedash.co.uk; native Mac app is coming." (Log every time this comes up — it's how we decide if the Mac app matters.)

## Log every conversation

Keep a simple table (add to this file or a sheet): date · garage · channel · what they use now · what it costs them · objection · outcome · follow-up date. Ten rows of this table is worth more than any strategy doc.
